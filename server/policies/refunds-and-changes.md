# Refunds and changes (generic India domestic demo)

## Refundable fares
Flex-type fares are refundable to the original mode of payment. For bookings canceled within 24 hours of purchase (and for departures beyond 7 days), more favorable refund rules may apply.

## Non-refundable fares
Saver-type fares are generally non-refundable, but statutory taxes and eligible airport charges may be refunded where required by regulation.

## How refunds are processed
Approved refunds are initiated to the original payment instrument used for booking:
- UPI / wallet: typically 1-5 business days
- Credit / debit card: typically 5-10 business days
- Net banking: typically 3-7 business days
Actual posting time depends on bank/payment partner processing cycles.

## Changes and reschedule
Date/time changes are allowed on Flex fares with fare difference. Saver fares may be changed for a change fee plus fare difference, if inventory is available.

## No-show handling
If a passenger does not report before departure (no-show), additional penalties may apply and full refund may not be available on low-fare categories.

## Involuntary disruption (delay, cancellation, misconnect)
When the airline cancels or significantly delays a flight, rebooking on the next available service or refund to original form of payment may be offered per regulation and ticket conditions. Keep boarding passes and any SMS/email notices for claims.

## Statutory consumer rights (summary)
Where applicable law grants cooling-off or mandatory compensation, those provisions apply in addition to airline tariff rules. This demo text is generic; always verify the notice printed on your ticket and the carrier’s conditions of carriage.
